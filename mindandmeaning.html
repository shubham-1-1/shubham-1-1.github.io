God and Godly

Look at Rama—a prince who accepted vanavāsa with grace, endured every trial in silence, and departed quietly after fulfilling his duty. Look at Krishna—the God of love, who could not stay with his parents, nor with the one he loved the most; loving deeply, yet always learning to let go, as if love itself was his sacrifice. Look at Shiva—the source of all creation, yet the possessor of nothing.

If you wish to truly feel the pulse of life, walk their paths. There, the deepest meaning of existence unfolds—and there lie true joy, peace, and purpose.

Life lives in emotion and finds meaning there. Before you act, ask your loved one: do they wish for a crook or an honest person? Ask your children: should I be manipulative or upright? Do not follow Ratnakara’s path of wrongdoing—choose Valmiki’s way of integrity, heart, and courage.

It is a miraculous universe, and God is magnanimous: everyone gets what they ask for. Thieves get their thefts, givers get their gifts—each receives the longings they bore.

The world is a stage filled with epic characters—Shakuni, Duryodhana, Dusshasana, Drona, Arjun, and even wallflowers. Who will you choose to be? Live the role you deeply admire.

Plant in devotion, and the harvest will be sacred.

Uphold righteousness and duty; good ultimately conquers evil: Ramayana

Dharma, when destroyed, destroys; Dharma, when protected, protects — Mahabharata

When righteousness declines and unrighteousness rises, the Divine manifests to restore balance — Bhagavad Gita

Uphold dharma through difficult choices; true victory lies in righteousness, not mere conquest — Mahabharata

It is not the Narayani Sena, but Narayan who matters.

Focus on the war, not every battle — the Pandavas won only 5 out of 18, yet they won the war.

True warriors remember honor—even in war. Ram paid tribute to Ravana; Arjuna bowed to his teacher Drona — Ramayana & Mahabharata

Truth wins over falsehood — Upanishad

Focus on the action, not the outcome. That is the path of peace — Bhagavad Gita

Nature sets the stage. Think right. Do right.

Those who walk the path of dharma never walk alone—the cosmos walks with them.

When all else fails, higher truth prevails.

To trust in God is to walk with integrity, even when the path is unseen.

When you’ve done your part, let go — and let God do His/Her.

No human holds the power to make or break you — let there be no confusion. Seek not their praise, but align your karma with the will of the Divine.

Sita could have returned with Hanuman, but she stayed. Because how you reach the end is just as important as reaching it.

Ramayana lives through Ram’s vanvas, not his rajya; Mahabharata endures through the Pandavas’ trials, not their throne. It's hardship that etches history, not ease.

It’s the process that truly matters, not the result. Right effort brings true value; wrong means, even if successful, are empty. So focus on the right path and leave the outcome in God’s hands.

Look back — those who wronged you are already facing the weight of their actions. Trust God, and stay true to good Karma.

At times, Lord Krishna's actions seemed unjust — yet they were always responses to deeper wrongs, to the cries of the helpless: Draupadi disrobed, Abhimanyu betrayed. Justice sometimes arrives in forms we don’t expect.

You trust a pilot you’ve never met—but doubt the Creator of the skies.

History is clear—from Ravan onward: eyeing what’s not yours only leads to ruin.

Ramayana isn’t about Ayodhya. Mahabharata isn’t about Indraprastha. Both epics turn on what happened in exile—far from the palace, close to truth.

If God does your work, who will live your life?

Arjun, the greatest warrior, paused often—not out of fear, but because greatness feels deeply.

The Pandavas never ruled the five villages they asked for—yet a war was fought for them. Sometimes, the fight is not for possession, but for principle: Mahabharata

Ram waited in the forest for 14 years. The Pandavas endured 13. Greatness waits — because time is the test every hero must pass.

Even the Mahabharata, the fiercest of wars, was bound by rules—because when dharma is forgotten, victory means nothing: Mahabharata

Greed leads to sin — Mahabharata

In Ramayana and Mahabharata alike, the war ends not in celebration, but in reflection—because Dharma always comes with a price: Ramayana and Mahabharata

Forgiveness is divine—but even Lord Krishna stopped at 100. Shishupal learned that too late.

Life and Living

The most beautiful things carry the marks of their journey. What is real is never polished — it is lived.

Read Feynman's biography (What Do You Care What Other People Think?) to see what a true scientist and professor is: heroic, humble, honest, humorous, wholehearted. Once you understand, you are part of that world.

Look upon the boundless cosmos and behold the power of its Creator—surrender every breath and dedicate every action in their name.

Build on your wounds and make them your grounds. Your scars are proof of the fire you survived.

Past words, present words, repeated words—listen carefully; they reveal the person.

A madman may hold the torch, but wisdom and courage in the right hands can still protect the village.

The seed sown today will bear fruit in its own time—just wait; karma whispers.

Offer even a scrap of authority, and integrity is traded for obedience—the East India Company’s tactic lives on.

Wisdom whispers of its limits; ignorance shouts of its certainty.

People who are behind you are behind you—respond with sympathy or ignore them.

Everything has a price. Take only what you deserve, or the dues will break you.

Energy cannot be stopped; it finds another path, guided by the universe to where it belongs.

When someone is truly good, their goodness spreads quietly, shaping the world for the better.

Look at the countless stars and the vastness of the Universe—imagine its unimaginable scale. It reveals the Creator's power. Surrender, and let the Creator guide your life.

Everything is conserved: what moves in shadow returns in shadow.

Not everything deserves your time—choose what serves your path.

When you live with goodness, the universe aligns to support you—even trials turn into gifts.

Live and let live.

Life hands you the pen. What you write becomes your story. Make it legendary.

The main role is never easy—but that’s where the real story is written.

Truth triumphs—never be disturbed by temporary falsehoods.

You live in your mind every moment—keep it clean and pure.

MJ had ordinary looks yet became a global icon. Messi has an ordinary build yet became the GOAT. Greatness comes from within.

Life isn’t about proving anything—it’s about living meaningfully.

In solitude, you meet the one who matters most—yourself.

A genuine friend helps you grow by saying no to what’s wrong.

Love everyone—not to gain anything, but simply because it makes your soul lighter.

Your gifts—be it knowledge, skill, power, or strength—aren’t meant for pride, but for a higher purpose: to serve those who lack them.

Power may not always be in your hands, but integrity is. Never serve what you know is unethical.

Just as you bath to cleanse your body, cleanse your mind every day, as often as you can, to wash away the dirt of thoughts.

What truly matters—love, time, peace—is never sold.

Burberry shows that greatness isn't in doing more, but in doing one thing—the iconic check—flawlessly.

Gazing at the sky is better than many things—spend your time wisely.

Let go of yesterday—today is a brand new beginning.

Don’t just live — leave a mark

In collective work, your actions may not change the outcome — but they shape your karma, and that shapes your life.

The flock finds safety in numbers; the eagle finds freedom in solitude

Let Bulgari grace your neck, Ferragamo guide your steps, Chanel shape your style — and a beautiful heart carry your soul.

It’s not talent alone that makes a champion, but the fire in their attitude.

They guard Messi like no one else — because he isn’t like anyone else. Opposition rises with your worth

Sledging might shake a moment — grace builds a legacy. Just ask Tendulkar.

Inner peace is silent strength — and that silence echoes where noise hides insecurity.

Sound fades. Feeling stays. Speak from where it stays.

Messi doesn’t block others — he just plays his game, silently, brilliantly, and lets the ball do the talking.

We change for the ones we love.

Clarity is time’s quiet gift.

Theatrics, rhetoric, and narratives don’t last. Truth, character, and consequence do.

The less you value yourself, the more you crave validation.

Power is a test. If it comes to you unearned, it’s more likely to expose than elevate you.

The lower they fall, the higher you rise.

In the end, people are brought down by the weight of their own wrongs — revenge is never necessary.

Gulab jamun may be your favourite — but try rasgola, and you might rethink everything.

Those at peace with themselves don’t need to rule anyone else.

Life knows its rhythm. Trust it. Let the story unfold.

Like humans, even stones have their luck — a rock lying unnoticed in the forest for centuries can one day become an object of worship. Everything has its time, its destiny.

The wiser you are, the less you judge.

Some people come into your life like angels — quietly, unexpectedly, and just when you need them the most.

Live like the main character—not a supporting role.

Time is equal; choices aren’t. Where one’s hours go, their truth flows.

If a lion starts chasing a mosquito on its tail — it’s no longer a lion, it’s a monkey.

Before you condemn the world, ask what you’ve allowed in your own space.

We begin life as thinkers — and end up as team players in someone else’s plan.

The stage shifts without warning — watch your step.

One who hasn't truly earned their place lives a borrowed life — udhar ki zindagi.

Time is the actual currency; all else are mere tokens.

Life grants you limited time; how you use it is your choice.

Time listens longer and reveals all truths.

If you have values, they're untouchable. If you lack them, they're unteachable.

Everyone is someone’s world. Don’t be the reason their world suffers.

If you move with the crowd, you may never find your own direction.

Not every reply needs a voice; some responses are best spoken through action.

A fighter’s real power reveals itself inside the ring, not on the streets.

If you’re surrounded by people who never disagree, you’re trapped in an echo chamber.

Life is a karmic account—every action has a reaction, and the balance is always kept.

Growing older is inevitable; growing up is a choice few make.

Accomplish first; preach later.

Nuisance power is taken; real power arrives uninvited, calm and unquestioned.

They foul. Tendulkar doesn’t complain — he answers with the bat.

The truly capable surround themselves with honesty, not flattery.

Lasting change doesn’t come through revolution — it comes through quiet evolution.

Mean what you say and say what you mean—otherwise, it's just noise dressed as words.

Every soul walks its own legend—some as warriors, some as wanderers, each wearing a mask of their own making.

What we are today is the result of yesterday’s actions; what we do today shapes tomorrow—so be mindful.

Whatever you say or do, you first create it within yourself—so be mindful of what you nurture inside.

Two hands hold only so much — be selective in what you carry.

The state of today’s world reflects the education we received—our system shapes who we become.

Before you criticize the world, ask yourself—are you doing your part?

Behind every moment lies a reason—nothing is truly random.

Go to either extreme, and you'll find emptiness.

What we choose reveals who we truly are.

Profession and Purpose

Positions may grant nuisance power, but true power comes from inner strength and moral authority.

You can't end all corruption, but you can end your role in it—by not doing it, and not enabling those who do.

Don’t get carried away by bombastic words and stylish accents—focus on content and depth – truth is simple and does not need any decoration.

Strength lies not in harming, but in choosing not to.

True power protects—it doesn’t destroy.

The crown doesn't make the king; the king gives meaning to the crown.

The strongest move in office politics is not to play the game.

To be a professor is to think deeply—that's where the real joy lies.

Critical thinking and fearless expression define a true professor.

Choose whose recognition matters—most don't.

Sometimes you must lose to truly win—but never win at the cost of what truly matters.

While they play their games around you, keep moving forward—your goal isn’t in their circle.

You weren’t born to do a job—you do a job to support a life worth living. Don’t lose sight of the difference.

Don't be disturbed by lies or waste time proving them; truth reveals itself as falsehoods unfold.

If you truly deserved something and didn’t get it, one day you’ll understand—it wasn’t meant for you, or it wasn’t good for you.

It's better to be quietly deserving than loudly questioned.

Raise your voice only when your heart speaks.

Don’t count the crowd—count the independent minds.

When the majority is right, authority is easy. When the majority is wrong, authority must stand apart—and stand up for the few.

The chair isn’t meant for passing the buck—it’s meant for carrying it.

Committees are often where the powerless feel powerful, while real agendas move quietly elsewhere.

Good governance doesn’t need many committees. It needs honest, capable leaders with courage.

Nepotism walks in first, recognition follows close behind, and plagiarism quietly takes a seat at the table — but don’t complain. Just watch how Messi dribbles past eleven and still finds the goal.

True worth reveals itself in silence — when people lean in and ask, “Who spoke just now?” If recognition must come with an introduction, and still leaves doubt, then the stature belongs to the title, not the person.

We live politics every day, yet few choose it as a path. Strange — for something so pervasive. If you do it, own it. Master it.

Titles don’t command real respect. Much of what appears is strategy, not sincerity.

When you choose to ignore what's right in your job, you're helping build the system your children will one day face.

Your children may never sit at your desk, but they’ll live in the world your choices built.

Street corners and boardrooms may look different, but both are arenas of belief, power, and persuasion.

Committees don’t exempt you from the laws of the land or nature. Power without wisdom comes at a price.

Whenever you favour someone, pause and ask—am I being fair to others who also deserve a chance? Unfair choices often return in unexpected ways.

Office politics may feel like power—until you see what it quietly cost you.

Don’t expect any place to be perfect — the sensible and the senseless are everywhere.

In a mad world, even hollow titles can make people jump.

Hollow minds measure others to feel full.

Just like humans, institutions too have their luck — sometimes they shine for decades, sometimes they wait in silence for their moment to arrive.

Put the wrong person in the right chair, and the chair itself looks wrong.

Even today, Eklavyas live unseen — deep in the jungles, far from the limelight. The ones we celebrate are no match for those quiet, forgotten gems.

Nobody likes exams, but they exist to measure how well you perform what you don’t enjoy.

Profession shapes both personality and perspective.

Nepotism wounds merit, but it quietly destroys those who practice it the most.

Nepotism blooms where insecurity takes root—when trust in talent is replaced by fear of being outshined.

People resort to abuse when they run out of reasons.

Silence is true indifference. Saying “I don’t care” often proves you do.

Ashwatthama is dead—truth twisted becomes the perfect lie: Mahabharata.

Every recognition costs something—be mindful of the price.

You can fool everyone—even yourself—but not God.

If you need a gang to take on someone, you've already lost the battle of character.

Whom you work with shapes not just your career, but your life.

You become the company you keep, slowly and silently. Choose carefully—your future walks with them.

Overachievement can cost your soul—underachievement may still keep it whole.

Degrees show education, not the heart or morals.

A goal's worth is defined by the opposition you face. If everyone on the field plays for you, it's no longer a goal—it's a handout.

Earn your wisdom through action—accomplish before you philosophize.

Research and Reflection

Leadership is not about being the greatest; it is about guiding the greatest — Oppenheimer did this on Project Y with Bohr, Fermi, and Lawrence.

Ridiculed by Eddington and dismissed by Bohr, Chandrasekhar and Feynman remind us that even the greatest opposition cannot outlast rigorous ideas.

The more you understand science, the more you begin to agree with the Shastras.

Climate science is becoming political science.

Both the Vedas and science teach this: do not trust blindly—verify through knowledge, inquiry, and direct experience.

Research means searching for the truth, so the most important quality of a researcher is how truthful they are.

Research trains the mind like a gym trains the body.

At its core, research is pure passion and mental exercise.

Even the unlettered can shine, when touched by grace and guided by perseverance.

Do not write so that people will read; write so that truth will be revealed.

Don't speak to impress—speak to express what must be said.

Better to stand still with purpose than to rush in the wrong direction.

Science loses its soul when it becomes a contest for recognition. Strip away the rewards, and what’s left is truth-seeking — pure, humble, and revolutionary.

A scientist’s mind seeks questions—not minutes and memos.

Science is a humble profession. If you're starving for the spotlight and chasing publicity, perhaps you're in the wrong field.

In statistics, we’re taught to question sample size, bias, and confidence. But in real-world decisions — in institutions, teams, or boards — we often settle for slim majorities. It’s a strange gap between what we learn and what we practice.

You chose science to think, not to pitch.

The way children see scientists is often worlds apart from the everyday reality of scientific work.

True PhD is earned in silence, struggle, and solitude.

PhD is a process that shapes the mind more than the manuscript.

Read more, write less—otherwise, it’s not knowledge, just noise and recycling.

Beauty lies in the eye of the beholder—not everyone can recognize everything. An ironsmith cannot recognize a diamond.

Your research topic, like your profession, quietly reshapes how you think.

Research is noble only when driven by truth—without it, it's just work.

Intellectual battles are the most thrilling sports—lasting years and decades, played 24/7.

The value lies in the content, not the style of speech.

Most of what we study fades in time—but what stays is the thought process we build along the way.
