Current Research Focus

Actionable Climate Information for Integrated Planning

— We generate climate intelligence for long-term planning and policymaking —

Global climate models exhibit substantial regional biases—reaching up to 25% in temperature and 50–100% in precipitation over South Asia—limiting their reliability for long-term planning.
This work develops scientifically grounded, regionally optimized climate frameworks that reduce these biases and produce credible climate intelligence for long-term planning and policymaking across sectors. The approach integrates physically based modeling with advanced statistical and machine-learning techniques to enhance regional fidelity and data reliability.
The resulting datasets provide substantially improved regional representation compared to widely available global climate products across many parts of South Asia.

Regional Climate Change: Processes, Hotspots, and Impacts

— Translating regional climate shifts into societal impact —

Examining how climate change manifests across South Asia through shifts in circulation, variability, and extremes, and identifying emerging regional hotspots. The work connects these physical changes to their implications for water resources, public health, economic systems, and renewable energy—providing an evidence-based foundation for long-term planning and policymaking.

Reorganization of the South Asian Monsoon

— Beyond variability, toward structural change —

Examining large-scale, multi-decadal restructuring of the South Asian monsoon system under anthropogenic forcing. Our work identifies systematic shifts in circulation, pressure gradients, wind regimes, and moisture transport—revealing how aerosols, greenhouse gases, and land-use changes are reorganizing the background state of the monsoon. These structural changes have cascading implications for rainfall patterns, hydroclimate extremes, water security, agriculture, energy systems, and regional policy planning.

Structural Foundations of Climate Modeling

— Physics, numerics, and the credibility of projections —

Developing and interrogating climate models at their structural foundations. Our research examines dynamical cores, numerical schemes, temporal resolution, surface topography representation, and parameterization frameworks to understand how model architecture shapes simulated rainfall, circulation, and monsoon dynamics. By diagnosing process-level biases and structural inconsistencies—including limitations of multi-model means—we aim to improve model fidelity and establish more scientifically defensible and actionable climate projections, particularly for vulnerable regions of the Global South.

Emerging Climate Risks and Systemic Challenges

— Responding to critical climate-linked challenges as they emerge —

Addressing urgent and emerging challenges at the intersection of climate, air quality, economy, public health, and development. While our core research focuses on monsoon dynamics and climate modeling, we engage with high-impact, climate-linked crises when scientific insight is needed to diagnose structural causes and inform systemic solutions. These contributions are problem-driven, interdisciplinary, and guided by long-term sustainability rather than short-term narratives.
